# Metrics and analytics

### Metrics philosophy

Metrics must measure the health of both sides of the marketplace.

Supply-side metrics track provider reliability and slot availability.

Demand-side metrics track user behavior and booking conversion.

Financial metrics track revenue generation and sustainability.

Operational metrics track system reliability and marketplace discipline.

Metrics must be simple during MVP but capable of expanding later.

Early metrics should focus on validating marketplace assumptions.

### Core marketplace KPIs

The most important marketplace KPI is successful booking volume.

Successful bookings represent validated demand and reliable supply.

Gross booking value represents the total value of transactions processed through the platform.

Platform revenue represents commission collected from successful bookings.

Active providers represent supply-side participation.

Active users represent demand-side engagement.

Balanced growth between providers and users indicates marketplace stability.

### Booking conversion funnel

The booking funnel measures how users move from discovery to confirmed booking.

Key stages include: Slot view – user opens slot details.

Booking attempt – user initiates checkout.

Payment success – Stripe payment is confirmed.

Booking confirmed – slot transitions to booked state.

Conversion rate between these steps reveals UX friction or payment issues.

Improving conversion rate increases marketplace efficiency.

### Supply side metrics (providers)

Provider supply metrics track the availability and reliability of providers.

Total providers represent overall marketplace supply.

Active providers represent providers currently publishing slots.

Slots published represent total available inventory.

Slots booked represent successful supply utilization.

Provider cancellation rate measures reliability.

Reliable providers increase marketplace trust and stability.

### Demand side metrics (users)

Demand-side metrics track user engagement and booking behavior.

New user registrations represent platform adoption.

Active users represent recurring engagement.

Booking frequency per user measures demand intensity.

Average booking value measures transaction quality.

User retention measures how often users return to book again.

Strong retention indicates marketplace usefulness.

### Financial metrics

Financial metrics track economic performance of the platform.

Gross Booking Value (GBV) represents total transaction value.

Platform revenue represents collected commission.

Average booking value indicates transaction quality.

Stripe fees represent payment processing costs.

Customer acquisition cost measures marketing efficiency.

Revenue growth indicates marketplace scalability.

### Provider reliability metrics

Provider reliability is a critical trust signal.

Cancellation rate is calculated as cancelled bookings divided by total bookings.

Reliability score may combine cancellation rate and booking history.

Providers with strong reliability improve user confidence.

Providers with poor reliability may require operational review.

Reliability metrics support future enforcement policies.

### Operational metrics

Operational metrics measure system and marketplace health.

Refund rate indicates supply reliability issues.

Chargeback rate indicates payment dispute risk.

Customer support volume measures operational friction.

System uptime measures platform availability.

Low operational friction indicates healthy platform performance.

### Early product market fit signals

Early signals of product-market fit include increasing booking volume.

Repeat bookings from users indicate strong demand.

Providers voluntarily publishing more slots indicates supply confidence.

Low cancellation rates indicate marketplace trust.

Organic growth without heavy marketing indicates strong product value.

These signals guide early product iteration decisions.

### Future analytics expansion

Future analytics may include advanced provider dashboards.

Predictive analytics may forecast slot demand.

Dynamic pricing analytics may optimize slot monetization.

Personalized recommendations may improve user discovery.

Marketplace analytics may guide expansion to new cities and categories.

Data-driven insights support long-term platform optimization.
