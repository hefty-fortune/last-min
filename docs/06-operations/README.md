# Operations and marketplace control

### Provider onboarding process

Providers must apply to join the platform.

Each provider application includes basic business information, service category, and location.

Providers must meet quality standards aligned with the platform’s premium positioning.

Applications are reviewed and approved manually during the MVP phase.

Approved providers receive access to the provider portal.

Rejected providers may reapply after improving eligibility criteria.

Manual onboarding ensures early marketplace quality control.

### Provider approval criteria

Providers must demonstrate strong brand positioning and service quality.

Providers must operate within targeted service categories.

Providers must maintain a professional online presence and customer reputation.

Providers must agree to platform reliability standards.

Providers must understand cancellation and payment policies.

Approval criteria ensure that the marketplace maintains premium perception.

### Slot publishing government

Providers are responsible for creating and publishing slots.

Slots must represent genuine last-minute availability.

Providers must ensure that published slots are actually fulfillable.

Providers should avoid publishing speculative availability.

Repeated publishing of unreliable slots may result in warnings or suspension.

Slot governance maintains trust in the marketplace.

### Cancellation monitoring

Provider cancellations are recorded and tracked.

Cancellation rate is calculated as the ratio of cancelled bookings to total bookings.

Providers with unusually high cancellation rates may be flagged for review.

Cancellation metrics may be visible to users as reliability indicators.

Repeated cancellations may lead to provider warnings or account suspension.

Monitoring ensures accountability and protects user trust.

### Marketplace trust enforcement

Trust is a core operational principle of the platform.

Providers must honor confirmed bookings.

Users must respect no-refund commitment rules.

Platform policies must be applied consistently across all participants.

Administrative intervention may occur when reliability thresholds are violated.

Operational discipline maintains the integrity of the marketplace.

### Abuse prevention

Providers may attempt to manipulate availability to attract attention.

Users may attempt fraudulent chargebacks or booking abuse.

Monitoring tools must detect abnormal booking patterns.

Provider reliability metrics reduce incentive for abusive cancellations.

Clear platform policies reduce ambiguity in disputes.

Abuse prevention mechanisms protect both sides of the marketplace.

### Manual intervention procedures

Administrators must have the ability to review problematic bookings.

Administrators may manually refund users when exceptional circumstances occur.

Administrators may temporarily suspend providers under investigation.

Manual intervention should remain rare and documented.

Operational logs must track all manual actions.

Human oversight is necessary during early marketplace growth.

### Customer support policy

Customer support must respond quickly to payment or booking issues.

Support must clearly communicate platform policies regarding refunds and cancellations.

Support interactions must remain professional and transparent.

Customer support should prioritize preserving user trust.

Support requests must be logged for operational analysis.

Early stages may rely on manual support workflows.

### Provider relationship management

Providers represent the supply side of the marketplace.

Maintaining strong provider relationships ensures reliable inventory.

Providers should receive clear communication regarding platform policies and updates.

Providers should be encouraged to maintain reliable cancellation behavior.

Provider feedback may inform future product improvements.

Healthy provider relationships increase marketplace stability.

### Operational scalability

Manual oversight is acceptable during the MVP phase.

As the platform grows, automated monitoring systems may replace manual review.

Operational dashboards may track cancellations, booking volume, and provider reliability.

Operational procedures must be documented to allow team expansion.

Scalable operations enable growth into new cities and service categories.
