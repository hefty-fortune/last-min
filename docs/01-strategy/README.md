# Vision and strategy

### Product vision

U zadnji čas is a curated last-minute availability marketplace for high-end establishments.

The platform allows premium service providers to publish short-notice openings and enables users to immediately confirm and pay for those opportunities.

The core idea is controlled scarcity with strict commitment.

The product is not discount-driven. It is opportunity-driven.

Users gain access to high-end services within short time windows.

Providers gain monetization of otherwise unused premium capacity.

The marketplace operates on first-come, first-serve logic with immediate payment confirmation.

Trust, reliability, and speed are fundamental pillars of the system.

The long-term vision is to become the dominant premium short-notice booking platform across major cities.

### Market positioning

U zadnji čas is positioned as a luxury last-minute access platform.

It is not a mass-discount marketplace like Groupon.

It is not a general reservation aggregator like Booking.com.

It is closer to a scarcity-driven exchange model where time-sensitive availability is monetized at premium or flexible pricing.

The experience must feel exclusive, curated, and high-trust.

The Zagreb MVP focuses only on high-end hair salons and restaurants.

Providers are selected and approved manually during initial phase.

Brand perception should communicate urgency without cheapness.

Scarcity is the core economic driver.

### Target customer

The target user is urban, financially capable, and values time over price.

The user is comfortable making quick decisions.

The user appreciates premium experiences and spontaneity.

The user is willing to commit immediately when opportunity appears.

The user understands that no-refund policy is part of accessing scarce availability.

The initial geographic focus is Zagreb city center and surrounding premium areas.

### Target provider

The target provider is a high-end restaurant/food and drink establishment, show/theater/concert venue, beauty/spa/hair salon, alternative/other service with strong brand positioning.

The provider experiences occasional last-minute cancellations or unused premium capacity.

The provider values monetizing empty slots without damaging brand perception.

The provider is willing to operate under strict reliability standards.

The provider benefits from immediate guaranteed payment and platform exposure.

The provider accepts visible cancellation tracking as part of maintaining marketplace trust.

### Competitive landscape

There is no dominant luxury-focused last-minute platform in Zagreb.

Traditional booking platforms focus on standard reservations, not scarcity-based monetization.

Discount platforms reduce brand perception and are not suitable for high-end establishments.

Direct provider Instagram or phone-based filling of cancellations is inefficient and unstructured.

U zadnji čas creates a structured, monetized, and scalable alternative.

Future competition may emerge from booking systems integrating last-minute modules.

Strong brand positioning and trust mechanisms will be key differentiation factors.

### Expansion vision (phase 2+)

Phase 2 may introduce bidding mechanics for selected categories.

Phase 2 may introduce provider financial penalties for excessive cancellations.

Phase 2 may integrate external booking system synchronization.

Phase 2 may expand to additional verticals such as medical, events.

Geographic expansion may include Split, Rijeka, Ljubljana, Vienna, and other regional urban centers.

Long-term vision includes becoming the reference platform for premium spontaneous bookings.
