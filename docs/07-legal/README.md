# Legal and compliance

### Legal framework overview

The platform operates as a digital marketplace connecting users and service providers.

The platform facilitates discovery, booking, and payment processing for last-minute service availability.

Service fulfillment is the responsibility of the provider.

The platform is responsible for payment handling, booking confirmation, and operational enforcement of marketplace rules.

Legal terms must clearly define responsibilities between users, providers, and the platform.

All users and providers must agree to platform Terms of Service before using the system.

### Terms of service (concept draft)

Users agree that bookings are final once payment is confirmed.

Users acknowledge that bookings cannot be cancelled for refund.

Providers agree to honor confirmed bookings.

Providers acknowledge that cancellations will trigger automatic refunds and affect reliability metrics.

Providers agree to maintain accurate availability when publishing slots.

The platform reserves the right to suspend accounts that violate reliability standards.

Terms of Service must clearly define dispute procedures and liability boundaries.

### Refund policy

User-initiated cancellations are not eligible for refund.

Provider cancellations trigger automatic refund to the user.

Refunds are processed through the original payment method.

Refund processing time depends on payment provider policies.

Exceptional refunds may be issued through administrative review.

Refund policy must be clearly communicated during booking confirmation.

### No-show policy

User failure to attend a booked appointment does not qualify for refund.

Providers are entitled to receive payout when users do not attend appointments.

Users are responsible for arriving on time for booked appointments.

The platform does not guarantee service quality beyond facilitating the booking.

Providers are responsible for delivering the service they advertise.

Clear no-show policy protects providers from last-minute user abandonment.

### Provider agreement

Providers must agree to platform operational rules.

Providers must ensure that published slots reflect real availability.

Providers must honor confirmed bookings.

Providers must accept automatic refunds when they cancel bookings.

Providers agree to maintain reliability standards defined by the platform.

The platform reserves the right to suspend providers who repeatedly cancel bookings.

Provider agreements ensure supply-side accountability.

### Stripe compliance

All payments are processed through Stripe.

Sensitive card information is handled exclusively by Stripe.

The platform stores only Stripe identifiers and references.

Stripe compliance requirements must be respected.

Payment disputes and chargebacks must follow Stripe procedures.

Stripe webhooks must be securely implemented and verified.

Stripe integration ensures secure and compliant payment handling.

### Consumer protection

The platform must comply with applicable EU consumer protection laws.

Users must be clearly informed about no-refund policies before completing payment.

Terms and conditions must be accessible and understandable.

Refund rules must not mislead users.

Data handling must comply with GDPR regulations.

Legal consultation may be required to validate policies in Croatian jurisdiction.

Compliance protects the platform from regulatory risk.

### Data protection and privacy

User personal data must be collected only when necessary.

Personal data must be stored securely.

Users must be informed about how their data is used.

Data access must be restricted to authorized systems and personnel.

Users must have the ability to request data removal where legally applicable.

Privacy policy must comply with GDPR requirements.

Protecting personal data maintains user trust and regulatory compliance.

### Chargeback handling

Users may attempt payment chargebacks through their card issuer.

Chargeback policies must clearly reference the no-refund booking rule.

Booking confirmation and terms acceptance must be recorded.

Evidence must be retained to defend against chargeback claims.

Chargeback monitoring must detect abnormal behavior.

Strong documentation reduces financial risk.

### Legal extensibility

Future product features may introduce additional legal requirements.

Bidding systems may require updated contract terms.

Provider financial penalties may require additional legal documentation.

Expansion to new countries may introduce additional regulatory frameworks.

Legal structure must be flexible enough to support future platform evolution.
